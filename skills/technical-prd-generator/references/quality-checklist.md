# PRD Quality Checklist

Before final output, verify the PRD satisfies these checks.

## Strategic Clarity

- The PRD has one clear recommended approach.
- The PRD explains why this approach fits the user's constraints.
- The PRD distinguishes goals from non-goals.
- The PRD does not overpromise outcomes outside the product's control.
- Any assumptions are explicit and reasonable.

## Buildability

- Functional requirements are specific enough for engineering.
- P0 requirements define a coherent MVP.
- Requirements use IDs and priorities.
- Technical architecture identifies major components and responsibilities.
- Failure modes and rollback/recovery paths are included for risky systems.
- Dependencies and integrations are named.

## Measurability

- Success metrics include concrete targets where possible.
- Acceptance criteria define when MVP is done.
- Performance budgets are measurable.
- Validation milestones include exit criteria.
- The test plan covers normal and failure scenarios.

## Safety, Security, and Trust

- Privacy/security requirements reflect the product's risk level.
- Sensitive data is minimized.
- Secrets and credentials have storage requirements.
- The PRD includes compliance, policy, or anti-abuse rules when relevant.
- The PRD includes user-facing recovery or support paths for risky changes.

## Research Quality

- Current or niche claims are researched and cited when tools are available.
- Technical claims rely on primary sources where possible.
- Citations support the exact claims they accompany.
- Research is translated into requirements rather than pasted as generic background.

## Output Quality

- Tables are readable and not overloaded.
- The document is long enough to be useful but not padded.
- Open questions are limited and actionable.
- The final decision is easy to find.
- The writing style is direct and senior, not vague or promotional.
